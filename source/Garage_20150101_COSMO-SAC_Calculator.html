Scientific Background & Model Description
Overview

The COSMO-SAC model predicts the non-ideal behavior of liquid mixtures by analyzing the interaction energies between molecular surface segments derived from quantum-chemical (QM) calculations. Unlike group-contribution methods, COSMO-SAC operates directly on the σ-profile — the molecular surface area distribution as a function of screening charge density σ — making it applicable to any molecule for which a COSMO calculation has been performed, without requiring fitted binary interaction parameters. It accounts for three distinct physical phenomena:

⚡

Electrostatic Misfit Energy

Segments with different charge densities repel one another. The misfit energy is proportional to α′ and the square of the sum of the two interacting charge densities.

🔗

Hydrogen Bonding

An additional attractive term activates when the acceptor segment charge exceeds σhb = 0.0084 e/Å² and the donor charge is below −σhb. Governed by the coefficient chb = 85 580 Kcal·Å⁴/mol·e².

📐

Combinatorial (Size & Shape)

The Staverman–Guggenheim term corrects for differences in molecular size (r = V/Vnorm) and shape (q = A/Anorm) using a coordination number z = 10.

Mathematical Workflow

Step 1 — σ-Profile Calculation

Raw QM segment charges are spatially averaged using a Gaussian-weighted kernel with an effective averaging radius Reff = 0.817642 Å. The averaged charge density for segment i is:

σnew,i = Σⱼ σⱼ · (Rⱼ²·Reff² / D) · exp(−d²/D)
─────────────────────────────────────────
        Σⱼ (Rⱼ²·Reff²·D) · exp(−d²/D)

where D = Rⱼ² + Reff²,  d = |rᵢ − rⱼ|

The averaged σ values are binned into a discrete histogram from −0.025 to +0.025 e/Å² in steps of 0.001, weighted by the segment area to give the σ-profile p(σ).

Step 2 — Segment Interaction Energy ΔW

The exchange energy between any pair of surface segments (σᵢ, σⱼ) decomposes into a misfit term and an H-bond term:

ΔW(σᵢ,σⱼ) = (α′/2)(σᵢ+σⱼ)²
          + chb·max(0,σacc−σhb)
                ·min(0,σdon+σhb)

σacc = max(σᵢ,σⱼ), σdon = min(σᵢ,σⱼ). The polarisation factor Fpol = (ε−1)/(ε+0.5) with ε = 3.667 scales α into α′ = Fpol·α.

Step 3 — Segment Activity Coefficients Γ

Solved self-consistently by iteration (convergence < 10⁻⁶) for both the pure species (SEGGAMMAPR) and the mixture (SEGGAMMA):

ln Γₛ(σᵢ) = −ln[ Σⱼ p(σⱼ)·Γₛ(σⱼ)·exp(−ΔWij/RT) ]

Successive substitution with damping:
Γnew = (Γcalc + Γold) / 2

Step 4 — Total Activity Coefficient γ

The final ln γ is the sum of the residual contribution (segment interactions) and the Staverman–Guggenheim combinatorial term:

ln γᵢ = Σₛ [pᵢ(σₛ)/Aeff]·[ln Γmix(σₛ) − ln Γᵢ(σₛ)]
       + ln(Φᵢ/xᵢ) + (z/2)·qᵢ·ln(Θᵢ/Φᵢ)
       + Lᵢ − (Φᵢ/xᵢ)·Σ xₖLₖ

Lᵢ = (z/2)(rᵢ−qᵢ) − (rᵢ−1),  z = 10
Module / File Structure

The original MATLAB codebase is organised into seven focused functions, all of which have been faithfully ported into the JavaScript engine of this tool.

MATLAB File Role
eqCOSMO.m Top-level driver for two-phase equilibrium; sets temperature and component list.
Binary.m Primary calculation loop — iterates over all mole fractions x₁ ∈ [0.005, 0.995] and returns the γ matrix.
SimgaProfileCalculator.m Manages the Gaussian segment-charge averaging and calls the sorting routine to produce p(σ).
Library.m Reads per-segment geometry (x,y,z), charge, and area data from inputQM.xlsx for a named compound sheet.
paraCOSMO.m Returns all universal COSMO-SAC constants: R, chb, σhb, Aeff, Vnorm, Anorm, α′, …
SortSimgaProfile.m Bins the averaged σ values into a discrete histogram from −0.025 to +0.025 e/Å² (step 0.001) weighted by area.
ConvertAU2A.m Converts QM output coordinates from Bohr (atomic units) to Ångströms via the factor 0.529177249.
Data Format & Usage Instructions

This tool is a direct browser replacement for the MATLAB workflow. The only prerequisite is your inputQM.xlsx file produced by a COSMO quantum-chemistry calculation.

Excel File Layout

Each compound occupies one sheet named after the substance (e.g. 67-63-0-2d). Required cells:

L2 → NUMSEGMENT (number of surface segments)
L3 → VolCavity (Å³) — COSMO cavity volume
L4 → AreaCavity (Å²) — COSMO cavity area
C4:C(N+3) → X positions (atomic units)
D4:D(N+3) → Y positions (atomic units)
E4:E(N+3) → Z positions (atomic units)
F4:F(N+3) → Charge (e)
G4:G(N+3) → Area (Å²)
H4:H(N+3) → Charge/Area = σ (e/Å²)
I4:I(N+3) → Potential

Workflow Steps

  1. Upload your inputQM.xlsx using the drag-and-drop zone above. The tool will automatically detect all compound sheets.
  2. Select Component 1 and Component 2 from the detected sheet names.
  3. Set the system temperature in Kelvin (default 298.15 K).
  4. Click Run COSMO-SAC. The engine will compute σ-profiles, build the ΔW matrix, iterate for segment activity coefficients, and evaluate γ at 991 composition points.
  5. Review the interactive charts (γ vs x₁, ln γ vs x₁, σ-profiles) and summary statistics.
  6. Click Export CSV to download the full results matrix equivalent to MixGamma.xlsx from the original MATLAB code.
💡 Tip: Infinite-dilution activity coefficients (γ₁∞, γ₂∞) are read directly from the Summary Statistics panel — γ₁ at x₁ → 0.005 and γ₂ at x₂ → 0.005.
